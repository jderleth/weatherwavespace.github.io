Investing in the Future of Orbit

Weatherwave is building a vertically integrated, rapid-production smallsat platform designed for the highest value space missions: weather, climate, environmental intelligence, and ISR. Our approach blends high cadence sensing, manufacturing efficiency, and continuous technology refresh into a scalable orbital service.

About Us Government Solutions

A Platform, Not a Mission

Most space companies build a single mission or a finite constellation. Weatherwave is building a platform. A rapidly produced, continually upgraded orbital sensing network that grows more capable over time and supports multiple markets.

The core asset is the production line itself: a modern American satellite factory capable of manufacturing more than one hundred spacecraft per year. This enables recurring revenue, predictable scaling, and high strategic value in a market where demand is accelerating.

A Rapid-Production Satellite Factory

Vertical integration is the foundation of Weatherwave's model. By controlling design, assembly, test, and integration, the company eliminates bottlenecks and unlocks scale.

Existing LEO operators assemble satellites for their own constellations, but none operate a fully productized, repeatable, high cadence line that can support external missions, hosted payloads, or new government requirements at speed.

Weatherwave is building the first system of this type in the United States, aligned to national demand and secure domestic supply.

High Value Markets

Government customers already spend billions per year on weather, climate, environmental monitoring, and intelligence data. These missions demand calibration, reliability, and performance not achievable with purely commercial imaging fleets.

Weatherwave is designed to meet government grade requirements while achieving smallsat scale economics. This creates a defensible position in high value, procurement driven markets that reward stability and long term performance.

Scalable Business Model

As the production line ramps, the company gains three scalable revenue vectors:

  • Data services for weather, climate, and ISR customers
  • Hosted payloads and rapid deployment for mission specific sensors
  • Platform-plus-production contracts for agencies that require on demand capability

This model allows for compounding returns as the constellation grows and as more partners rely on Weatherwave for rapid access to orbit.

Built for Long Term Strategic Value

The next decade will see thousands of new small satellites launched, but production capacity inside the United States remains limited. Weatherwave fills a critical national gap: a secure, high tempo, American production line dedicated to resilient sensing architectures.

The combination of vertical integration, high cadence manufacturing, and dual use mission applicability positions Weatherwave as a durable infrastructure company with both commercial and government relevance.
