Commercial Solutions From Orbit

Weatherwave is designing an orbital sensing network that serves real decisions on the ground: agriculture, insurance, environmental risk, and geospatial analytics. The same architecture that supports national missions can power commercial insight at scale.

About Us Investor Overview

Designed For Real Operators, Not Just Demonstrations

Many commercial users of space data still fight with delayed deliveries, inconsistent coverage, or products that were never tuned to their decisions in the first place. Weatherwave takes a different approach. We start with government grade sensing requirements, then shape data products that match the cadence and reliability commercial partners need in the field.

Agriculture and Food Systems

Producers, cooperatives, and agtech platforms need more than a single snapshot of a field. Weatherwave aims to provide frequent, calibrated views of moisture, heat stress, and crop condition so that:

  • Growers see problems before they spread
  • Advisors can recommend targeted interventions
  • Supply chain partners gain earlier insight into regional yield and risk

The goal is simple: better data that supports better decisions across a growing season.

Insurance and Risk Analytics

Insurers and risk analytics firms need consistent, objective views of exposure before, during, and after events. With a high cadence orbital network, Weatherwave intends to support:

  • Pre event exposure mapping for portfolios and regions
  • Near real time impact assessment for hurricanes, floods, and fires
  • Long term trend analysis for climate driven risk

Government grade measurements can anchor actuarial models, parametric products, and new classes of climate linked services.

Infrastructure, Energy, and Environment

Operators of critical infrastructure and energy systems need wide area awareness, not just local sensors. Weatherwave is being built to support:

  • Monitoring of corridors, coastlines, and key facilities
  • Environmental compliance and condition tracking over large regions
  • Support for situational awareness during extreme weather and outages

The same architecture that serves national missions can give operators a broader, more stable view of their systems.

Working With Weatherwave

Weatherwave is in the early stages of building its constellation and its production line. We expect the most valuable commercial partnerships to come from close collaboration with a small number of initial design partners.

Over time, we anticipate three main ways to work together:

  • Data services that deliver stable, well calibrated products on predictable schedules
  • Joint product development where Weatherwave sensing is tuned to specific use cases
  • Hosted payload opportunities when a custom instrument makes sense for both sides

Today, Weatherwave is focused on building the core architecture and validating the most important use cases. As the system matures, we intend to open structured pathways for commercial partners to plug in and grow with it.
